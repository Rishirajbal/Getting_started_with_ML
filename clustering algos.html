K-Means Clustering

K-Means is a centroid-based algorithm that partitions data into K distinct clusters based on distance metrics. It works by iteratively assigning points to the nearest centroid and recalculating centroids until convergence.

Strengths: Simple, efficient for large datasets, works well with spherical clusters.

Weaknesses: Requires specifying K, sensitive to initialization, struggles with non-spherical clusters.
